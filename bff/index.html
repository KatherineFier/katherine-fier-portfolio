Case Study:
The Boulder Flower Farm

Overview

The founders of an established wholesale flower and herb farm located in Boulder County, CO wanted a new reccuring revenue stream. This new revenue stream became the Boulder Flower Farm (BFF), a high-end wedding venue business. A new brand, website and marketing strategy was needed.

The Process

This project included 5 phases: Research and Branding, Visual Design, Website Build, Marketing Strategy and Implementation, and Customer Experience.

The 5 Phases

Phase I: Research & Branding

The first phase of the BFF project was focused on understanding the product: specifically, identifying what made the venue unique and who the target client would be.

Market Research

During this phase, I engaged in extensive market research including: interviewing the founders and grower, interfacing with their existing customers, participating in daily farm operations, researching competing venue spaces, and speaking with vendors who had previously participated in putting on events at the farm.

Tools Used: Excel, Google, Wedding Wire, The Knot, Instagram, Ride Alongs

Phase II: Visual Design

To develop the visual brand identity, I completed three steps: Choosing the Brand Colors and Fonts, Creating the Brand Name and Tagline, Designing the Logo and Favicon.

Step 1: Choosing the Brand Colors & Fonts

Given the product (mid to high-end wedding venue), target clientele (engaged 25-35 year old females), and nature of the farm (lots of vibrant floral colors); I intentionally chose a neutral, modern and feminine color palette and fonts. Keeping with a neutral color palette was important because I wanted the colorful flower-filled photography to take center stage. I did not want the brand colors to distract from the photos we would use to showcase the farm’s vibrant, natural beauty.

For the fonts, I chose a feminine Script font to be used in the logo, a classic Serif font for headers (a nod to the historical element of the farm) and a Sans Serif that would be modern and easy to read for the primary text.

Tools Used: Illustrator, Google Fonts, Google Search

Step 2: Creating the Brand Name

Ideating the brand name, the Boulder Flower Farm, was simple after the extensive research completed during Phase I. With the intention to communicate what the product was, and why it was so special - I came up with the name “The Boulder Flower Farm” and the tagline “A True Colorado Venue.”

Tools Used: N/A

Step 3: Designing the Logo & Favicon

For the Logo and Favicon, I chose Dancing Script (a script font) and Raleway (a sans serif font). I intentionally used the script font as the primary logo font because it felt feminine, beautiful and classic. And I used a bit of Raleway font in the logo to add a modern edge.

I chose the Golden brown color to use in both the logo and favicon in order to reflect the luxurious aspect of the brand. I did not use any illustrations in the logo, but did incorporate “est 1983” to reflect the longevity, and authenticity of the farm (one of the USPs identified in Phase I). The date also added a classic and trusted feel to the logo.

Tools Used: Illustrator, In Design, Moo.com

Phase III: Website Design

There were five steps to create the website. First, was identifying the site goals. Next was choosing a website platform. Then was designing the UX and UI. And, lastly, was completing the Copywriting.

Step 1: Identifying the Site Goals

As with any website, I started the web design process by meeting with the team to identify the primary site goals. Did they want to sell flowers on the website? Build an email list? Or drive people to their social media? Together, we landed on the following primary site goal: for site visitors to inquire about booking the venue through the contact page.

Tools Used: Website Questionnaire

Step 2: Deciding on a Website Platform

The parent company website was built on Wordpress, which was a poor fit for the company. The owners had difficulty making small, but necessary updates to the site. And so the site became outdated quickly. Consequently, the criteria for choosing a platform for the new website was the following: must be simple to make copy and images updates, no need to manage security issues, no need to make technical updates to maintain the functionality of the site.

Tools Used: N/A

Step 3: Designing the UX:

To create a website that would engage site visitors, create demand and ultimately an inquiry - I strategically set up the: Site Map, Header and Footer Navigations, flow of each page, and Calls To Actions (CTAs).

The Site Map included the following 12 pages: Home, Venue, Flowers, Praise, Contact, About, Gallery, Blog, Events, FAQ, Legal, and Privacy. I limited the Header Navigation to the following four pages: Venue, Flowers, Praise, and Contact. I set up the Header Contact page link to be a button so it would stand out and capture the site visitor’s attention. The remaining 8 pages listed in the Site Map, were not critical to achieving the site goal and so I included them in the Footer Navigation.

I focused the Homepage, the most important and frequently visited page of the website, on clearly communicating What BFF Is and Who It Serves. To do this, I strategically placed photography followed by short, clear copy and specific CTAs.

The first banner image on the Homepage as well as the short intro copy clearly communicate What BFF is and Who it’s for. Each subsequent Homepage section highlights one of the Unique Selling Propositions identified in Phase I via both the Image and Copy, and includes a clear CTA to drive the site visitor to learn more about the Venue and ultimately send an inquiry.

Tools Used: Wireframing, Squarespace

Step 4: Designing the UI:

It was important to keep the UI, clean, modern and feminine; So that the site would appeal to BFF's ideal customer and embody a high-end, boutique feel. To achieve this, I focused on incorporating lots of white space and being consistent with when and where I used specific fonts and colors.

The biggest challenge when it came to creating the UI was the photography. The owners had some professional photography, but there were large gaps in the images needed to effectively showcase the property. This required that I create a shot list and find a local photographer to fill in these gaps.

Tools Used: Squarespace, HTML, CSS3, Google

Step 5: Copywriting:

To write effective website copy, I kept my writing clear, short and conversational. I focused on speaking directly to BFF's ideal customers addressing their needs, hopes and desires. And then relating these back to BFF and what it uniquely had to offer.

The most significant challenge for me was integrating foundational copywriting strategies with the heart and essence of the farm. Due to the research I completed in Phase, I had a well of first and second hand stories to draw on. And so I focused on weaving these into different parts of the website. A good example of integrating effective copywriting principles with the essence of the farm is the Meet the Grower page. You can check it out here!

Tools Used: N/A

Phase IV: Digital Marketing

The BFF marketing strategy included five methods. And was an ongoing process of creating a plan, testing it, and making optimizations based on budget, available resources, and what the market did and didn't respond to.

There were two initial challenges. First, the advertising budget was less than $10k. And, second, was getting stakeholder buy-in to implement digital marketing in addition to traditional marketing methods.

Here's the initial plan I implemented:

Method 1: Marketing Collateral and Farmers Markets

The first action item was to inform the audience that TFHC was already interfacing with about BFF. This would require no advertising money and little no additional time or personnel.

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator & In Design

Method 2: Wedding Wire and The Knot Listings

The second method was to create a wedding venue listing on both Wedding Wire and the Knot. Wedding Pro (the parent company) estimated significant traffic to our listings, and one booking would pay back the investment. Covid hit the month after we signed up. And so many couples were not looking to book venues for the 2020 season. BFF booked at 50% capacity the first wedding season, which was also Covid.

Tools Used: The Knot, Wedding Wire

Method 3: SEO

There were three key pieces to my SEO strategy. Keyword research, on-page SEO and off-page SEO. I utilized Google search and MOZ.com to create a list of keywords. For on-page SEO, I focused on integrating these keywords into the BFF website copy and URLs. For off-page SEO, I focused on link building with relevant external sites such as venue directories, and local event planners. I also created and maintained a Google My Business account.

Tools Used: Google Search, Google Analytics, MOZ.com

Method 4: SEM

The keyword research I did for SEO was also critical for SEM. I drew on both this research and the brand research I completed during Phase I to incorporate the 5 USPs into the copy for each paid ad. We tested a variety of copy and targeting based on age, sex, interest and location. And found that X performed best.

Tools Used: Google Search, Google Analytics, Google Ad Words, MOZ.com

Method 5: Social Media & Partnerships

I focused in on one social media platform, Instagram, and used it strategically. Instead of going for quantity of followers and frequency of posts, I built the account slowly and intentionally as a virtual gallery, storefront and business card. This strategy proved to be a powerful networking tool for building brand/business partnerships.

Tools Used: Instagram, Facebook, Planoly

Method 6: Reviews

Reviews are an ongoing and integral part of the BFF marketing strategy. They enable BFF to establish a deeper trust with site visitors and potential customers quickly. Knowing this, I carefully and thoughtfully follow up with each client who has an event at the farm asking for feedback, and if they had a positive experience at the farm, for a review as well.

Tools Used: Google My Business, Wedding Pro, Illustrator

Phase V: Customer Experience

The customer experience is a critical part of BFF. Embodying friendliness, kindness, generosity, professionalism (being timely and consistent, and paying attention to details) are all vital aspects of the brand. To ensure these values were woven into each customer interaction, I co-wrote and/or copy-edited each customer touch point including the: website copy, marketing copy, customer response templates, sales contracts, and house information/rule book, thank you notes and product labels. I also co-created physical products and used them to create on-brand custom gift packages for each client.

Hydrosols Project

To further create a valuable and memorable customer experience, I co-developed custom hydrosols made with lavender and herbs from the farm to gift BFF clients. For this project, I completed product research and wrote clear, compelling copy for six product labels and three product descriptions.

Tools Used: Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Google Suite

Results

Developed a new brand (The Boulder Flower Farm) and grew a new line of revenue, increasing the parent company's overall yearly business profitability by 20%. Fully booked out the venue, the first and second season post-Covid. Established a 6% website conversion rate.
